One of the first questions every homebuyer asks is "how much can I afford?" The honest answer is — it depends on more than just your income. Here's how lenders actually look at it and what you can realistically expect in San Antonio's 2026 market.

## The Rule Lenders Actually Use

Lenders don't just look at your salary. They look at your **debt-to-income ratio (DTI)** — the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward monthly debt payments including your new mortgage.

Most loan programs allow a DTI up to 43–50% depending on the loan type and your overall financial profile. VA loans and FHA loans tend to be more flexible on DTI than conventional loans.

## A Simple Starting Point

Here's a rough guideline for San Antonio buyers in 2026:

- **$50,000/year income** — buying power roughly $150,000–$180,000
- **$75,000/year income** — buying power roughly $220,000–$270,000
- **$100,000/year income** — buying power roughly $300,000–$360,000
- **$150,000/year income** — buying power roughly $450,000–$530,000

These are estimates assuming moderate existing debt and current interest rates. Your actual number could be higher or lower depending on your credit score, down payment, and monthly obligations.

## What Affects Your Buying Power

**Credit score** — a higher score gets you a lower rate, which means more home for the same monthly payment.

**Existing debt** — car payments, student loans, and credit card minimums all reduce how much mortgage you can carry.

**Down payment** — a larger down payment lowers your loan amount and eliminates or reduces mortgage insurance.

**Loan type** — VA loans offer $0 down with no PMI, which significantly increases buying power for eligible veterans.

## San Antonio's Market in 2026

San Antonio remains one of the more affordable major metros in Texas. Median home prices hover around $280,000–$320,000 depending on the area, which puts homeownership within reach for a wide range of buyers.

Compared to Austin or Dallas, your dollar goes further here — and with the right loan program, many buyers are surprised by how much they can actually afford.
