Most people have never heard of USDA loans — but for buyers purchasing in certain areas around San Antonio, it's one of the best kept secrets in mortgage lending. Zero down payment, competitive rates, and no PMI in the traditional sense.

## What Is a USDA Loan?

The USDA Rural Development loan is a government-backed mortgage program designed to encourage homeownership in rural and suburban areas. It's offered through the U.S. Department of Agriculture and provides 100% financing — meaning no down payment required.

Despite the name, USDA loans aren't just for farmland. Many suburban neighborhoods qualify, including areas around San Antonio that might surprise you.

## What Areas Around San Antonio Qualify?

USDA eligibility is based on location and population density. Many communities surrounding San Antonio fall within USDA eligible zones including areas like:

- Floresville
- Pleasanton
- Seguin
- Castroville
- Hondo
- Parts of Converse and Von Ormy

The USDA updates its eligibility maps periodically so the best way to know if a specific property qualifies is to check directly — which I can do for you in minutes.

## Who Qualifies for a USDA Loan?

To qualify you need to meet both property and income requirements:

**Property requirements:**

- Must be in a USDA eligible area
- Must be a primary residence
- Must meet basic property condition standards

**Borrower requirements:**

- Household income cannot exceed 115% of the area median income for your county
- Stable employment and income history
- Credit score of 640 or higher preferred
- U.S. citizenship or permanent residency

## What Are the Benefits?

- **Zero down payment** — 100% financing means you keep your savings
- **Competitive interest rates** — often comparable to conventional loans
- **No traditional PMI** — there is a guarantee fee but it's typically lower than FHA mortgage insurance
- **Flexible credit requirements** — more accessible than conventional loans for buyers with average credit

## How Does It Compare to Other Zero Down Options?

If you're a veteran, the VA loan is still the gold standard — no down payment, no mortgage insurance at all, and funding fee exemptions for veterans with service-connected disabilities. But for non-veterans who don't have a down payment saved, USDA is often a better option than FHA because the mortgage insurance costs are lower over time.

## Is a USDA Loan Right for You?

If you're open to living in the suburbs or smaller communities around San Antonio and your household income is within the limits, a USDA loan could get you into a home with zero out of pocket on the down payment.
