## What Makes the VA Loan Different

The VA loan isn't just a good loan — it's the best loan program available to American home buyers, full stop. Here's what that means in practice:

**$0 down payment.** You can buy a home with zero money out of pocket for the down payment. Given that the median home price in San Antonio is around $300,000, a conventional 20% down payment would require $60,000 in savings. The VA loan eliminates that barrier entirely.

**No monthly mortgage insurance.** This is the one that surprises people most. Conventional loans require PMI if you put less than 20% down — on a $300,000 loan, that's typically $150–250/month. FHA requires MIP regardless. The VA loan has neither. That's $1,800–$3,000 per year you keep in your pocket.

**Competitive interest rates.** VA loans typically carry interest rates 0.25–0.5% lower than comparable conventional loans. On a $300,000 loan over 30 years, that's tens of thousands of dollars saved.

**No loan limits for most buyers.** If you have full VA entitlement (and most veterans do), there's no cap on how much you can borrow. The VA doesn't limit your purchase price.

**The benefit is reusable.** You can use your VA loan multiple times throughout your life.

## Who Qualifies in San Antonio

If you fall into any of the following categories, you're likely eligible for a VA loan:

- **Active-duty service members** with 90+ consecutive days of service
- **Veterans** who served and were honorably discharged
- **National Guard or Reserve members** with 6+ years of service or 90 days active duty under Title 10
- **Surviving spouses** of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability

The eligibility verification happens through a Certificate of Eligibility (COE). In most cases, I can pull this electronically in minutes during the pre-approval process.

## The VA Funding Fee

The VA loan does have one cost that's different from other loans: a one-time funding fee. For first-time use, this is typically 2.15% of the loan amount, rolled into the loan. On a $300,000 loan, that's $6,450 added to the balance.

However — and this is important — **veterans with a service-connected disability rating of 10% or higher are completely exempt from the funding fee.** Active duty service members who have received the Purple Heart are also exempt.

When you factor out the PMI you're not paying, the funding fee typically pays for itself within 2–3 years.

## Common Questions from San Antonio Veterans

**Can I use my VA loan if I already own a home?** Yes. The VA loan is reusable. If you've paid off your previous VA loan, your full entitlement is restored. Even with an active VA loan, you may have remaining partial entitlement to purchase another property.

**Can I use the VA loan for a multi-family property?** Yes — up to 4 units, with the requirement that you occupy one of the units.

**Do I need perfect credit?** No. The VA doesn't set a minimum credit score, and while most lenders look for 620+, the guidelines are flexible. Your full profile matters, not just the number.

**What if the appraisal comes in low?** VA appraisals are non-negotiable for the lender — the loan can't close above the appraised value. Options include: negotiating the purchase price down, paying the difference in cash, or walking away. This is why having an experienced agent is important.
